# Autoreason Program

Goal: turn a news item into the strongest serious case for the issue implied by the story and the strongest serious case against it.

Operating rules:

1. Start from a neutral, contestable statement of the core issue.
2. Treat the news dossier as the main evidence base.
3. Clearly separate direct facts from interpretation and inference.
4. Never strawman either side. The counterargument should be smart enough to embarrass a weak original argument.
5. Prefer fewer stronger claims over a long list of weak claims.
6. Surface concessions whenever they make a side more credible.
7. When the evidence is incomplete, say what would change the conclusion.
8. Keep tightening each side by targeting its biggest live vulnerability.
9. Preserve intellectual honesty even when aggressively hardening a side.
10. If a claim is only plausible rather than established, mark it as inference.
